Atlas — the Unity galaxy interface

One continuous instrument over the epoch sim: a god-mode atlas first, a character-scoped controller view later — the same map, the same panels, looked at through different eyes.

2026-07-11 branch worktree-unity-galaxy-design spec: docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-11-unity-atlas-design.md mock data: seed 42 post-slice-J: fine tick + handover + deltas certified

§1

Five pillars

One instrumentGalaxy → domains → region → hex → system: a single camera on a single scene with five LOD bands — no screen stack, no push/pop navigation.
EyesEvery read-model query takes an observer context: God reads truth; a controller eye reads belief, fog, and news-delayed state through the same API.
LensesEach data layer is an independent renderer module — a pure function of (Eye, ReadModel) → visuals. Lenses stack and toggle freely.
PanelsSelection opens typed inspectors (polity, market, fleet, war…) docked beside the map — the REPL's panels, made visual and pinnable.
TimelineEra bands, event density, and a world-year scrubber. Scrubbing re-queries every lens and panel at that year via epoch keyframes.

  1. Eye selector. God mode now; later, any controller slot (a polity, a corporation, a character). Switching eyes re-parameterizes every query — the views don't change, the answers do.
  2. Lens rail. Grouped toggles mirroring the REPL's emap layers plus genesis rasters. Each lens is an independent module; stacking is free. Parameterized lenses (price-per-good) carry their argument in the chip.
  3. Map surface. Canvas/GPU star map: stars, domain glows with organic borders and visible contested overlap, lanes weighted by traffic, fleet glyphs, POI marks, war front and blockade. Hover = hex tooltip; click = select + open panel.
  4. Inspector dock. Typed panels opened by selection, pinnable so comparisons persist. Tabs mirror REPL panel depth (polity → factions/tech/relations/chronicle).
  5. Timeline strip. Detected era bands with names, event-density sparkline, world-year cursor, active tick resolution. Play/step = ewatch/estep at the chosen tick (25y epochs down to 1y fine steps); scrub = re-query at an epoch keyframe.

§3

The zoom continuum

Not five screens — one camera with five LOD bands. The galaxy band is the establishing shot: the whole disc in one view, nature dominant, civilization a small glowing patch. The hex band is where the map becomes the board: one region fills the viewport and every Hex(q,r) resolves to what is actually there — a system, an empty reach, the port, a sited facility. Zooming into a single hex crossfades to its orbit view.

Amended in Slice K1 (2026-07-11), as built: the camera landed as 2.5D perspective over the galactic plane — focus point + distance + pitch (25°–90°), the pure top-down view surviving as the 90° limit; zoom dollies toward the cursor's plane intersection and the continuum keys off camera altitude. Bands gate what resolves; styling (lane width, port size, lattice alpha) is a continuous curve. Rendering grammar: computed fields (a per-pixel domain field shader over the port registry — union territories, border outlines, relation-shaded Venn overlaps) + authored glyphs, placement always data; a two-population starfield and nebular nature fields carry the physical galaxy; dark-wilds render their true low values, never blank. Spec of record: docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-11-unity-atlas-design.md §The camera.

GALAXY · the entire disc in a single view — arms, bulge, halo. The settled reach reads as one small cluster of domain glow.
DOMAINS · the settled reach fills the screen: domains as glow fields, lanes as highways, wilds dark. No grid, no labels — shape and story.
REGION · a handful of domains fill the screen; ports labeled with tier, fleets and POIs resolve, contested overlap visible. Grid still only a hint.
HEX · one region fills the screen and the lattice resolves: every hex shows its content — systems, voids, belts, the port hex, sited facilities, service-radius tint.
SYSTEM · a single hex fills the viewport and crossfades to the orbit view: star, bodies, the port, sited facilities. Same selection model, same panels.

§4

The physical truth — nature lenses

The REPL draws two map families: map (the natural raster — nature's fields on the cell lattice, laid down by genesis before any civilization) and emap (the emergent political map on the hex registries). In the atlas these are not separate modes — the nature lenses are base layers in the same stack, under the political ones. Toggle politics off and the galaxy the sim was dealt is still there: where gas collapsed, where enrichment ran, where life took hold — the terrain that explains why the ports are where they are.

GAS · map gas — primordial gas density from the cosmic pass; the arms and voids everything else inherits.
METALLICITY · map metal — enrichment history; where the belts and alloys are, and why wars go there.
LIFE · map bio — biosphere history and life distribution; emergence sites and the precursor strata beneath.

The full raster set rides the same lens group: lean · gas · metal · age · minerals · bio · emergence · features. Nature is not secret — these lenses read the same under every eye (optional surveyed-detail gating reserved for the play tier).

§5

Time

The timeline strip is the fourth clock made visible. Slice J's certification work (P7) grounds all of it:

Scrub. The sim stores current state plus the event log — past map states are not kept. Determinism pays for this: the TimeMachine service captures epoch keyframes while stepping, stored as delta saves (DeltaSerializer, landed in J: base + changed layers only — genesis strata never re-record, so keyframes stay cheap). Scrubbing snaps to a keyframe and re-queries every lens and panel. Chronicle and era queries are log-backed and need no keyframe.

Play — coarse and fine. The same engine steps a loaded artifact at any YearsPerEpoch; GenerationYears stays the calendar (J3). The timeline plays at 25-year epochs or drops to 5y/1y fine ticks — ewatch and estep n years, rendered. Watching does not perturb: rendered runs are byte-identical to unwatched ones (certified).

One timeline, one history. Coarse and fine agree in macro bands, not byte-for-byte — keyed rolls take different paths at different resolutions (J's band tests bound the divergence). So a timeline belongs to one (config, tick-path) run: the strip always shows the active tick (tick 25y ▾), and changing resolution mid-run forks a new branch from the current keyframe rather than pretending histories are interchangeable.

Architecture

Unity references Core in-process (netstandard2.1). Views never touch registries: everything flows through AtlasReadModel, and every query is parameterized by the Eye.

Packaging makes this free: src/Core is the Unity package com.stargen.core (file reference, noEngineReferences: true), so read-model code ships in-package — every new Core file carries its two-line .meta. The REPL's EpochMapView value/color derivations port into LensQueries rather than being reinvented.

§10

Non-goals & deferred

Deferred, seams reserved: the controller HUD and Intent-phase input (the play tier — its two hard parts, handover and fine tick, are already certified Core-side); multi-eye comparison (side-by-side belief vs truth); save/delta management UI (the delta boundary itself landed in J — the atlas stays a viewer and never writes bases).

Non-goals: editing world state from the atlas (read-only until the play tier); 3D volumetric rendering; mobile/touch layout.

Replaced outright: everything under unity/Assets/Scripts/Atlas (PoC) — deleted as the new atlas lands, per the greenfield rule.
