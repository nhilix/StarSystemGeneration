## 1. Overview

The L0 goods layer: a 17-good vocabulary in three tiers with 1–4-step chains and
recipe variants, the **Grade** system carrying quality from geography through
manufacturing to retail, a segmented demand model that gives every good its value
(P4), a per-polity **legality** overlay creating black markets, a 14-type
infrastructure catalog with siting rules and a production formula, and port-hosted
market geography. Fixes the exotics-deficit dead code at the root: exotics are
pulled by tech, components, medicine, and luxuries against site-concentrated supply.

## 2. Decisions

- **Medium chains** (user selection over short-chains and flat goods): 17 goods,
  recipes 1–4 steps deep; some processed goods directly consumable, the most
  valuable goods multi-step. Distinct raw roots: Provisions, Ore, Volatiles,
  Organics, Exotics — Volatiles/Composites cash in 0a's mineral-vs-gas geography.
- **Multiple recipes per output with grades** (user requirement): standard
  (exotics-free, mass) vs advanced (exotics-gated, higher grade base) variants for
  Machinery, Armaments, Components; tech tier gates variants. Exotics scarcity
  reads as capability asymmetry, not just deficit.
- **Grade codified as first-class substrate** (user adjudication — codify before
  proceeding): stocks are `(quantity, grade)`, grade ∈ [0,1]; terrain-rooted origin
  (enrichment → ore purity, biosphere richness → provisions quality); processed
  grade = recipe base × input blend × facility tier × tech tier; **tech tier is the
  grade ceiling; precursor artifacts sit above any current ceiling** (why ruins are
  prizes). Quantity-weighted mean blending, consumption at mean, no FIFO. One
  interface: `Effective(useCase) = quantity × GradeMultiplier(useCase, grade)`.
  Grade × price gives value density (long-haul trade self-selects high grade);
  grade × war gives quantity-vs-quality forces; grade × play-clock drives retail
  item availability/quality sampled from local wholesale stocks.
- **Illicits via a legality overlay** (user addition): legality is per-polity
  policy (legal/restricted/prohibited + tariffs), never a property of the good;
  prohibition converts demand to black-market demand served by smuggling (off-lane
  physics). Narcotics added as a good; **sentient trafficking modeled as an illicit
  population flow**, not a stockpiled good — flagged crime against the population
  substrate, maximally reputation-damaging.
- **New goods** (user additions): Composites (Volatiles+Organics — plastics/chem
  derivatives), Narcotics, Compute (Refined Exotics+Composites — tech multiplier,
  automation, advanced ships, machine-population consumption).
- **Play-clock projection**: one vocabulary, two zooms — retail items are instances
  of wholesale categories, generated by sampling local (good, grade, quantity);
  personal equipment ← Armaments/Consumer Goods, augmentation ← Medicine+Compute,
  habitation ← Machinery/Composites, vessels ← Components; career goods are
  services priced off the same markets (P7 applied to goods).
- **14-type infrastructure catalog** in five families (Keystone: Port; Extraction:
  Mine/Skimmer/Agri/Excavation; Processing: Refinery/Chemworks/Fabricator/Exotics
  lab; Heavy: Foundry/Shipyard/Arsenal/Compute core; Support: Depot/Fortress);
  closed versioned vocabulary; tiers 1–3; build costs in real goods; every facility
  a hex-anchored pre-commitment. Ownership/condition lifecycles owned by pass 2;
  war-objective role by pass 5.
- **Production formula**: `base(type,tier) × terrain(raster) × labor(domain pop ×
  affinity) × machineryGrade × automation(compute)`, plus an **organic baseline**
  (facility-less subsistence — unserviced systems are poor, not starving).
- **One market per port** (follows the space amendment): state per (market, good) =
  price, cleared quantity, mean grade, + black book. Lane-connected markets
  arbitrage within freight capacity; disconnection diverges prices (blockade =
  visible price spike); wilds have no market; prices are perception-gated (P3).
  Price formation mechanics deferred to pass 2.

## 3. Testing Strategy

- **Invariants**: recipe conservation (outputs never exceed input mass-equivalents
  per recipe definitions); grade always ∈ [0, ceiling(tech)]; blending
  quantity-weighted; no facility without a valid siting hex; one-anchor-per-hex
  honored; black-book demand ≤ prohibited-demand scale.
- **Unit tests**: grade propagation through a 4-node chain; variant recipes produce
  the documented grade bases; Effective(useCase) monotone in grade; retail sampling
  reproduces local grade distribution.
- **Acceptance bands** (reference config, once integrated): production mix per good
  within bands; exotics genuinely scarce (importing polities exist); price spread
  across connected vs disconnected markets; facility counts by family in range.
- **Goldens**: reference goods table + a frozen production/market summary.
