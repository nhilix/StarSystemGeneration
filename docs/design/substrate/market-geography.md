# Market Geography

Where markets live, who participates, and what state they carry. Price *formation*
mechanics (clearing, elasticity, drift) belong to the economy layer
([../economy/markets.md](../economy/markets.md)); this document pins the geography.

## One market per port

The domain market sits where the port's service area meets the lane network. Its
state, per good: **price, last-cleared quantity, mean grade** — plus a **black
book** for goods prohibited under local law (thin volume, high margins,
smuggler-supplied; see legality in [commodities.md](commodities.md)).

## Participants

- **Domain population** — demand by band (subsistence / standard-of-living /
  luxury), embodiment- and culture-modulated.
- **Local facilities** — sell output, buy recipe inputs and upkeep.
- **Polity procurement** — military goods, construction, stockpile targets.
- **Corporations and traders** — arbitrage, contracts, cornering.

## Connectivity is price structure

Lane-connected markets arbitrage toward each other within freight capacity;
disconnected markets diverge. A blockade appears as a **price spike** at the
strangled port and a glut at the cut-off producer — the price map is the most
legible economic layer the atlas renders. The wilds have no market: barter and
self-supply only, which is mechanically why joining a domain matters to a settled
system.

## Prices are perception-gated

Actors know prices only as fresh as traffic brings them (P3). Stale-price
arbitrage is real at the generational clock and is a trader player's edge at the
play clock.

## P1 evidence

- **Legible residue**: the price-map layer (per-good shading with spikes at
  blockades and gluts at cut-off producers); black-book presence marks lawless
  ports.
- **Inhabitable state**: the player trades against real market state — buys the
  glut, runs the blockade, exploits stale prices ahead of the news.
