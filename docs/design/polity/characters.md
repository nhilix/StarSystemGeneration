# Characters

Individuals — sparse by construction, generated on demand, biographical by
derivation (P8).

## Sparsity

A character exists only when an institutional **role** needs an occupant or an
**event mints a notable**. Role slots come from institutions: polity leadership
(shaped by government form — ruler, council head, heir, marshal), faction leaders,
corporate executives, fleet commanders. A polity carries perhaps a dozen living
characters.

## Generation

Deterministic on demand from (institution, culture, species, seed): a name in the
culture's syllable flavor, age, lineage link, and a **personality** — a position
in ideology space plus four scalars: **boldness** (risk appetite), **zeal**
(ideology intensity), **competence** (execution quality), **ambition** (self over
institution). Personality enters institutional decisions through the temperament
composition's ruler term — and equally for factions, corporations, and fleets
through their leaders.

## Lifespan and succession

Aging is species-real: human-analog decades; lithic centuries (the Steward
Dynasty's basis); hive continuity (the hive is the character — functionally
immortal, personality drifting); machine minds that fork or deprecate instead of
aging. Death checks per epoch (age curve + hazards: war for commanders,
assassination risk scaling with faction militancy × own ambition × unpopularity).
Death triggers the government form's succession rule; a contested succession is a
**succession crisis** — resolved as politics or escalating into the coup /
civil-war machinery.

## Dynasties

Lineages tracked where forms care (dynastic autocracies, steward dynasties,
merchant houses). Dynastic prestige accumulates across reigns and feeds
legitimacy. **Cross-polity dynastic ties** (marriages, wardships) are a diplomacy
instrument consumed by the inter-polity layer — shared lineage shifts stances, and
succession claims can cause wars.

## Notables

Threshold events mint characters: a great battle → **war hero** (often straight
into a commander slot); a founding convoy → **founder**; a sacral surge →
**prophet** (who may seed the faction that follows); piracy concentration →
**pirate lord**; a corporate boom → **magnate**; a ruin expedition →
**explorer**. Notables are capped per polity; they take roles, patronize
factions, or carry chronicle color and die famous.

## Renown and arcs

Characters accrue renown from events they participate in; renown feeds faction
patron strength, ruler legitimacy, and commander weight. Every character has a
**derivable biography** — born, rose, led, fell — reconstructed from the event
log with no extra authoring: P8's character-scale story test.

At play clock the same characters resolve continuously; the player occupies a
character-scope controller slot among them (P2); NPCs deepen on demand
deterministically. Intrigue beyond ambition-driven hazards (spy networks, plots)
is deferred depth — the state model supports it.

## P1 evidence

- **Legible residue**: rulers, admirals, and prophets are named chronicle anchors
  with biographies; the polity panel shows the reign.
- **Inhabitable state**: characters are patrons, rivals, and employers at play
  scope — and the player is one of them.
