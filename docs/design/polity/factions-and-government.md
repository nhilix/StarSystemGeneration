# Factions, Government & the Temperament Composition

The politics layer: how interests organize, pressure, and — sometimes — become new
institutions; what forms governments take; and how a polity's decision personality
composes.

## Faction formation (P5)

A faction coalesces when a coherent interest diverges from how the polity is run.
Six bases: **ideological** (a popular cluster far from official ideology),
**cultural** (a minority culture segment), **regional** (frontier domains far from
the capital's care), **corporate** (dividend-fed elites), **military** (veteran
and commander networks), **sacral** (faith movements).

Faction state: basis, **strength** (population share + wealth + patron
characters), **agenda** (policy deltas: law changes, war or peace, autonomy, the
throne), militancy.

## Pressure

Each Interior phase, factions push: polity policies drift toward strong factions'
agendas (bounded); appeasement spending buys factions off (a real budget line);
unappeased strong factions accumulate **grievance**. The Intent AI feels this as
weighted pull; a polity-scope player feels it as demands to meet or defy.

## Graduation

When `strength × grievance` beats the polity's grip (`legitimacy × enforcement`):

| Basis | Graduation | Result |
|---|---|---|
| regional / cultural | **Schism** | domains secede as a new polity — culture, ideology, name from its own segments |
| throne-seeking | **Coup** | leadership replaced, ideology lurches; contested → civil war (the war machinery, against a provisional polity) |
| economic | **Charter** | the merchant faction incorporates ([../economy/corporations.md](../economy/corporations.md)) |
| any, failed | **Revolt** | crushed graduation: unrest damage, repression, martyrs — compounding grievance |

## Legitimacy and cohesion

**Legitimacy** = f(SoL trend, official-vs-popular ideology gap, war outcomes,
ruler prestige, cultural accommodation). **Cohesion** = legitimacy aggregated
across segments, discounted by structural strain (size, culture count, capital
distance). Low cohesion accelerates faction growth and lowers graduation
thresholds — conquest empires carry their successor states inside them, visibly,
faction by faction.

## Government forms

A closed catalog seated in ideology space × species:

| Form | Seat | Succession | Character |
|---|---|---|---|
| Autocracy | Authority + Individual | dynastic/designated | ruler-weighted; suppresses until it snaps |
| Collective | Authority + Communal | committee selection | inertial, stable, slow |
| Assembly | Autonomy + Open | election cycles | absorbs faction pressure via politics; lurches at elections |
| Syndicate | Material + Individual | boardroom | corporate-dominated; wealth is legitimacy |
| Theocracy | Sacral + Authority | doctrinal | faith is legitimacy; dogma constrains policy |
| Hive Unity | hive species | continuity | cohesion floor; near-immune to factions |
| Machine Consensus | machine species | none | near-zero ideology drift; chillingly consistent |
| Steward Dynasty | lithic longevity | very rare succession | century-reigns; deep inertia |

Form sets succession rule, policy inertia, faction tolerance, and what generates
legitimacy. Forms change **through graduation events** (a coup makes an autocracy,
reform under pressure makes an assembly) — each a chronicle landmark.

## The temperament composition

A polity's Intent-AI personality =
**species disposition × official ideology × ruler personality × faction
pressure**, with weights set by government form (autocracy: ruler dominates;
assembly: popular ideology dominates; hive: species dominates). The same nation
turns aggressive under a new ruler, mellows as prosperity drifts its ideology
open, or is dragged to war by a military faction it cannot afford to appease.
This replaces any fixed species temperament vector.

## P1 evidence

- **Legible residue**: the polity panel shows form, legitimacy, and factions with
  strength bars; coups, schisms, reforms, and revolts are chronicle landmarks.
- **Inhabitable state**: factions are employers, patrons, and conspiracies a
  character can join; a polity-scope player is the thing they pressure.
