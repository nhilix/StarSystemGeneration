# Ships & Fleets

*Status: awaiting design pass (3). This stub states scope only.*

The fleet model — the physical carriers of goods, power, information, and influence.
A shared component consumed by both the economy (freight) and inter-polity dynamics
(war); owned by neither.

Defines, when designed:

- Ship classes and classifications (civilian, corporate, military, state), with
  species- and culture-flavored design lineages: hive ships, machine swarms and
  AI-controlled vessels, dreadnoughts, carriers, freighters.
- Production chains: shipyards consuming real goods via recipes; construction time;
  maintenance and attrition.
- The fleet as the unit of tracking: composition, ownership, movement, positioning,
  reach; freight capacity per route per owner (the capacity interface markets
  consume).
- Information carriage: news and perception updates travel with traffic; propagation
  speed emerges from shipping density.
- Commanders: admirals and captains as characters in fleet roles; notable fleets and
  flagships as chronicle anchors.
