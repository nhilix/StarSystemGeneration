# War

War fought with fleets over infrastructure, caused by politics as often as
economics, ended when the loser's politics break.

## Causes — tension discharges through a casus belli

Wars require a declared goal (legibility: "the Ore War of epoch 6" — now with a
full menu):

| Category | Examples | Sourced from |
|---|---|---|
| **Economic** | resource seizure (deficit/price shock on a good the target produces), chokepoint/lane control, punitive response to interdiction | prices |
| **Ideological** | crusade (ideology gap × zeal, sacral surge), liberation of cultural kin, containment of a rising incompatible power | identity layers |
| **Political** | succession claims, military-faction grievance discharge, vassal enforcement or secession | factions, dynasties |
| **Spark** | border incidents (patrol clashes in overlap zones, smuggler-enforcement killings, refused reparations); escalation odds ∝ tension | the space model |

The **spark mechanism**: incidents roll continuously in high-friction space; one
escalates to war only when tension is loaded and a viable casus belli exists.
Low-tension incidents fizzle into demands and apologies (also events).
Declaration is an Intent act carrying an objective set plus a settlement demand.

## Conduct — the theater/objective model

A war is a set of **objectives**: ports (siege/capture), lanes (blockade),
domains (occupation), enemy fleets (destruction), plus defensive mirrors. Each
epoch:

1. **Assignment** — each side's Intent posts fleets to objectives per doctrine
   (temperament composition) and commander personality (bold admirals
   over-extend, cautious ones preserve hulls).
2. **Engagement resolution per objective** — attacker vs defender **fleet
   vectors** (strike vs screens, swarms vs tracking), modified by fortification
   (Fortress facilities), **supply** (fuel/upkeep reach from home ports —
   extended lines degrade readiness and are raidable objectives themselves),
   commander competence, and seeded rolls. Outcomes: decisive / attrition /
   stalemate; hull losses become wreckage at the hex; facilities take condition
   damage; captures transfer intact.
3. **Sieges** — blockade plus assault at a port; duration structured by defender
   reserves, fortress tier, and relief attempts. A fallen port transfers its
   domain **with population segments intact** — conquest composition is
   automatic.

**The war economy runs through everything already built**: armaments and fuel
drain from markets; war loans finance deficits and leave overhang; **weariness is
the interior responding** — losses and SoL decline shift population ideology,
feed peace factions, erode legitimacy. A polity breaks when its politics break.

## Termination and settlement

Break conditions: political collapse (legitimacy/factions), fleet/stockpile
exhaustion, capital loss, extinction (extinct belligerents fight no fronts; both
extinct → white peace). Settlement is **negotiated from per-objective outcomes**:
cede objectives, reparations/tribute, vassalization, imposed legality (forced
open ports), or white peace. Each AI accepts when the *perceived* cost of
continuing exceeds the settlement — and perception is stale (P3), so wars can run
past their rational end because one side doesn't yet know it's losing.

## Aftermath — residue is first-class

Lost-territory grudges persist as standing claims (tomorrow's tension); veterans
strengthen military factions; war heroes mint; wreckage fields and razed
facilities become POIs; debt overhang shapes the postwar decade; and **conduct
reputation** — atrocities, starvation sieges, honored surrenders — travels the
news graph and reprices every stance toward the actor.

## P1 evidence

- **Legible residue**: war layers on the atlas (objectives, blockades, supply
  lines); named battles and sieges in the chronicle with commanders and dates;
  settlement treaties as records; wreckage and ruins at the hexes where it
  happened.
- **Inhabitable state**: joinable wars, escort and blockade-running contracts,
  letters of marque, sieges the player can run supplies into — and at polity
  scope, the same objective assignments the AI makes.
